# Bit Rot

**Bit Rot** (or data rot) refers to the slow deterioration of data integrity on storage media. 

## Single Event Upsets and Causes
Because digital information is physically stored as microscopic electromagnetic charges (on HDDs, NAND flashes, or RAM), external forces can inadvertently invert these charges—causing a "bit flip".

- **Cosmic Rays**: High-energy subatomic particles from outer space continually bombard the Earth. A precise strike on a volatile memory chip can spontaneously change a `0` to a `1`. Anecdotal anomalies, like unexplained system crashes (Blue Screens of Death), a bizarre voting count surge in a Brussels election, or mathematically impossible glitches in a Super Mario 64 speedrun, are often attributed to cosmic ray bit flips.
- **Material Degradation**: CD/DVD optical layers oxidize and degrade over decades. Premium storage strategies often utilize M-Discs (made with non-reactive inorganic rock-like substrates) to ensure millennium-scale integrity.
