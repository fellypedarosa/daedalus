# Fabio Akita

Fábio Akita is a prominent figure in the Brazilian software engineering community, creating dense, highly technical content centered around computer science fundamentals, historical timelines in computing, and modern infrastructure.

## Content Philosophy
His approach demystifies high-level abstractions by grounding them in first principles, such as explaining modern gaming emulator constraints through the lens of 6502 processors, memory maps, and hardware limitations. 

He maintains an extensive personal infrastructure, including NAS arrays and homelabs, often pushing the boundaries of Linux networking, containers, and GPU utilization for both AI testing and gaming. Recent workflows involve using models like [[Claude_Code|Claude Code]] to define infrastructure as code.
