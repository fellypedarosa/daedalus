# Career Development in IT

Professional growth in technology is a combination of foundational knowledge, observed experience, and the ability to manage risk and complexity.

## Academic Specializations

The choice of degree impacts the depth of foundation vs. market readiness:

- **Computer Science (CS)**: Applied Mathematics. Focuses on the "why" and "how" of computation (Von Neumann architecture, Compilers, OS internals, Distributed systems). High value for the long-term as the foundations rarely expire.
- **Software Engineering**: Focuses on building economically viable technical solutions. Emphasizes product development, viability, and engineering process.
- **Computer Engineering**: Focuses on Hardware/Software interaction. Circuits, microprocessors, and lower-level logic.
- **Information Systems**: Focuses on business automation and data processing. More practical for the enterprise sector (ERPs, business logic).

## Experience Levels (Seniority)

Seniority is not just time; it is a measure of confidence, reputation, and decision-making capability.

### The Junior
- **Definition**: Little or no observable results in the specific industry context.
- **Focus**: Learning from mistakes.
- **Success Metric**: Not repeating the same error twice and being "smart enough" to be supervised.

### The Mid-Level (Pleno)
- **Definition**: Can solve problems with 10% of the attempts a Junior needs.
- **Danger**: **Dunning-Kruger Effect**. A cognitive bias where one overestimates their ability because they possess enough knowledge to solve common problems but lack the perspective to see their deeper inabilities.
- **Sign of Growth**: Realizing that 20% of your knowledge is excellent but 80% is still lacking.

### The Senior
- **Definition**: Trusted decision-maker with "Skin in the Game."
- **Value**: The ability to choose the right path and assume ownership of the outcome (success or failure).
- **Scalability**: A Senior scales **horizontally** by mentoring others.
- **The 10x Developer**: Usually not a coder who writes 10x more lines, but a Senior who enables 5 Juniors to perform like 5 Plenos by guiding them past common pitfalls (multiplicative effect).

## Career Strategy: "Surfing the Curve"

Technology adoption follows an **S-Curve**:
1.  **Early Adopters**: High risk, specialized knowledge.
2.  **Early Majority**: Rapid growth. This is the optimal time to "surf" (becoming Pleno/Senior right as the market demand peaks).
3.  **Late Majority / Laggards**: Technology becomes a commodity. Salaries stagnation and over-saturation.

**Strategy**: Diversify your "career portfolio" like a financial one. Keep a core in proven "fixed income" technologies (SQL, C, Java) and invest a small portion of time in "high risk" early-curve technologies (new paradigms, languages).

## Professional Habits
- **Peer Review**: Essential for all levels. "No code should be immune to revision." It enforces quality and accelerates the growth of Juniors while keeping Seniors honest.
- **Skin in the Game**: Avoid taking technical advice from people who don't suffer the consequences of those decisions.
- **Long-term Thinking**: Invest in knowledge with a half-life of 10-20 years (Computer Science fundamentals) rather than current framework hype.
